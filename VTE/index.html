Venous Thromboembolism

Venous Thromboembolism

VTE is a condition that is caused by a blood clot stuck in a vein, this can cause a loss of blood flow to a certain region in the body.

There are two types of VTE: “Deep vein thrombosis (DVT) Deep vein thrombosis is a clot in a deep vein, usually in the leg. DVT sometimes affects the arm or other veins. Pulmonary embolism (PE) A pulmonary embolism occurs when a DVT clot breaks free from a vein wall, travels to the lungs and then blocks some or all of the blood supply.

Pulmonary embolism (PE) A pulmonary embolism occurs when a DVT clot breaks free from a vein wall, travels to the lungs and then blocks some or all of the blood supply.Blood clots originating in the thigh are more likely to break off and travel to the lungs than blood clots in the lower leg or other parts of the body.” (heart.org)

Many different things can cause VTE such as surgery, cancer, a stroke, hospitalization or immobilization.

People who are at risk of getting VTE include:

- Older people

- People who are overweight

- People with thicker blood

- People with cancer or other cancer like diseases.

- People with genetic blood clotting

- Increased Smoking

- Family history of this disease

VTE is also one of the most preventable diseases in hospital. VTE is more common in hospitals because of increased confinement to a bed. Since it is more common in hospitalized patients hospitals need to find a way to ensures that there is no blood clot in the patient’s leg because it could lead to the clot traveling to the lung where it would cut of some of the person’s air supply.

Symptoms

- Pain in the infected leg

- Swelling in the infected leg

- Red or discolored skin

- A feeling of warmth in the infected area

To protect yourself from PE get plenty of exercise and try to move around. Decrease your smoking habits and lose some weight

Postphlebitic syndrome

This is a syndrome that can happen after you have had VTE, this can cause some major damage to the blood clot which can impact blood flow in the impacted areas. This can cause: Skin discoloration, Leg pain, Skin sores and constant swelling of the leg.
